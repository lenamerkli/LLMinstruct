     - [Transfeminine Science](https://transfemscience.org/) (also has relevant info for transmasc HRT)

   - **Understand the risks**:
     - **Testosterone** can increase red blood cell count (risk of clots/strokes), affect cholesterol, and strain the liver if not monitored.

   - **Common**: Acne, mood changes, increased body hair, voice deepening, bottom growth, fat redistribution.

     - Chest pain, shortness of breath (possible clot or heart strain).
     - Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes), dark urine (liver damage).
     - Extreme mood swings/depression.

### 7. **Long-Term Considerations**
   - **Fertility**: Testosterone can reduce or stop ovulation. If you may want biological children, consider egg freezing *before* starting HRT.
   - **Bone health**: Long-term testosterone use may affect bone density (ensure adequate calcium/vitamin D).
   - **Cancer risks**: Limited data exists, but monitor for unusual lumps or changes.
