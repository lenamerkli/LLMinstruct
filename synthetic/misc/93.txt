Great that the logarithmic scale worked!

The issue you’re seeing — the y-axis line appearing at **x=1** instead of **x=0** — is expected behavior when using a logarithmic x-axis. Here’s why and how to fix it:

---

### Why does this happen?

- On a **logarithmic scale**, the value `0` is undefined (log(0) → -∞), so the axis **cannot** be placed at `x=0`.
- By default, `axis lines=middle` places the y-axis at `x=0`, but since `0` is not on the log scale, it defaults to the closest valid position, which is `x=1` (log(1) = 0).
- So the y-axis line appears at `x=1` — the "middle" of the log scale.

---

### How to fix it?

You have two main options:

---

#### Option 1: Place the y-axis at the left edge (lowest x-value)

Instead of `axis lines=middle`, use:

```latex
axis x line=bottom,
axis y line=left,
```

This places the y-axis at the **left edge** of the plot (at the minimum x-value, e.g., 0.01), which is valid on a log scale.

---

#### Option 2: Manually specify the position of the y-axis line

You can use the `axis line style` and `extra x ticks` to fake the y-axis at a different position, but this is more complicated and usually unnecessary.

---

### Recommended fix: change your axis options to

```latex
\begin{axis}[
    axis x line=bottom,
    axis y line=left,
    xlabel={price in USD},
    ylabel={lmarena.ai score},
    enlargelimits=0.1,
    legend style={
        at={(0.5,-0.18)}, anchor=north,
        legend columns=2, column sep=1em,
        draw=none, font=\small
    },
    width=\textwidth,
    height=0.3\textheight,
    xmode=log,
    log ticks with fixed point,
]
```

---

### Summary:

| Before                      | After                         |
|-----------------------------|-------------------------------|
| `axis lines=middle`          | `axis x line=bottom, axis y line=left` |
| y-axis at x=1 (log scale)    | y-axis at left edge (x=0.01)  |
