§u§
Simple inline table in latex
§a§
Here are several simple ways to create an **inline table** in LaTeX (i.e., a small table that appears within a paragraph of text rather than floating).

### Method 1: Using `\small` + `tabular` inside a paragraph (most common)

```latex
Here is an inline table:
{\small
\begin{tabular}{||c|c|c||}
\hline
Year & Temp (°C) & Rainfall (mm) \\
\hline\hline
2020 & 15.2 & 820 \\
2021 & 15.8 & 790 \\
2022 & 16.1 & 850 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
}
that shows the trend.
```

Result: The table appears right inside the sentence, no floating, no caption, no numbering.

### Method 2: Even smaller and truly inline (no line breaks)

```latex
The results are
\begin{tabular}{c|c@{\ }c}
  & \textbf{Mean} & \textbf{SD} \\[2pt]
A & 45.3 & 3.2 \\
B & 48.1 & 2.9
\end{tabular}
and show a clear difference.
```

### Method 3: Using `inline` option with `float` package (prevents floating)

```latex
\usepackage{float}  % in preamble

The summary is shown in Table~\ref{tab:inline}:
\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
Method & Accuracy & Time (s) \\
\midrule
SVM    & 92.3\%   & 12.4 \\
RF     & 93.1\%   & 8.7  \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Small inline comparison}\label{tab:inline}
\end{table}
with random forest performing best.
```

(The `[H]` from the `float` package forces the table to stay exactly where you place it.)

### Method 4: One-liner with `array` (very compact)

```latex
Results: $\begin{array}{c|c}
\text{Group} & \text{Score} \\ \hline
X & 87 \\ Y & 91
\end{array}$
```

Use the first or second method for most “inline table” needs — they’re simple, don’t require extra packages, and stay perfectly inside the paragraph.
