What happened on June 4th, 1989 in Poland?
§a§
On June 4th, 1989, a significant event took place in Poland: **the first partially free elections after decades of communist rule.** 

These elections were a pivotal moment in Poland's transition to democracy. 

Here are some key points:

* **Partial Freedom:** While not fully free and fair, these elections allowed for opposition candidates to run against the communist party for the first time in many years.
* **Solidarity's Victory:** The Solidarity trade union, led by Lech Wałęsa, won a landslide victory in these elections, securing a majority of seats in the newly formed parliament.
* **End of Communist Domination:** This electoral success marked a significant blow to the communist regime and paved the way for the eventual collapse of communism in Poland and across Eastern Europe.


The June 4th, 1989 elections are considered a landmark event in Polish history, symbolizing the beginning of the end for communist rule and the dawn of a new era of democracy.
